Chicken Teriyaki- Do it yourself!

Like I said, I'm taking a stand. We all deserve better than where our culinary decisions have most likely led us. For me that's regretting the 20$ down payment to panda express at 2 AM or compensating for my homemade chicken chops with a half bottle of teriyaki left in my fridge.

Now that brings to me the sauce. This has been my sweet saving grace for so damn long, and its due time to pay her some respects. I'll show you how to take the job into our hands.

Don't mind the fact my own cooking career barely extends past Kraft mac n cheese... I believe in second chances.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tbsp Garlic Paste
  • 4 tbsp Light Soy Sauce
  • 3 tbsp Mirim
  • 3 tbsp Sake
  • 1/2 Cup Pineapple Juice
  • 2 tbsp Dark Soy Sauce
  • 5 tbsp Brown Sugar
  • 2 tbsp Sesame Seeds
  • 1 tbsp Corn Starch 2 tbsp water (Slurry)
  • And of course, fill a cutting board with skinless, boneless chicken thighs

Sauce Steps:

  1. Set your pan on simmer
  2. Squeeze garlic paste into pan, stir it with a spoon
  3. Add light soy sauce, sake, mirim, pineapple juice, and dark soy sauce. Mix.
  4. Add brown sugar. Mix.
  5. Add corn starch slurry and sesame seeds.
  6. Mix well and pour into a saucepan.

Chicken Steps

  1. Slice each chicken thigh into chunks so sauce can seap in well
  2. Grab salt and pepper to season your chicken
  3. Seal bag chicken into a few ziplock bags without air
  4. Drop into pot of water
  5. "Use the “water immersion” method. Place the food in a zipper lock bag and slowly lower the bagged food into a bowl of water, letting the pressure of the water press air through the top of the bag. Once most of the air is out of the bag, carefully seal it just above the water line."
  6. Clip the bags to the side of the pot
  7. Set sous vide cooker to 167F for 2 hours
  8. Cool in refrigerator for 10-15 min
  9. Paper towel the chicken to dry it
  10. Fill pan with vegetable oil until it smokes on high
  11. Add chicken in without crowding the pan and turn the heat down to medium
  12. Sear each side for 2 min with a spatula
  13. Get 'em on a plate, add sauce, and serve!
